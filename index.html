About

Inclusive measurement and theory for fertility-related distress

My approach blends co-design with people with lived experience and clinicians, qualitative synthesis, and psychometric validation (EFA/CFA/SEM). The goal is robust tools that improve clinical care and make research findings comparable across studies, clinics, and populations.

Research Focus

What I work on

  • Measurement of fertility-related distress (content validity, readability, invariance)
  • Conceptual domains: emotional, cognitive/rumination, identity/meaning, stigma/social legitimacy, treatment burden, autonomy/power, uncertainty/loss
  • Mixed-methods methodology & open science
Current Project

FRD measure & integrative model

I am developing an inclusive, psychometrically robust measure and an integrative model of fertility-related distress (FRD). To protect participant privacy and scientific priority, detailed materials (e.g., item wording, scoring keys) will be released after peer-reviewed acceptance or registered milestones.

Broadly, the work combines co-design (with people with lived experience and clinicians), qualitative synthesis, and psychometric validation (EFA/CFA/SEM). Initial public outputs will include a concept summary and protocol overview; full instrument release will follow acceptance with a clear license and citation.

Open Science

Transparent & staged sharing

All studies will be time-stamped via preregistration. Early registrations and project materials are held private until peer-reviewed submission or acceptance, at which point public components (protocols, analysis code, de-identified data where appropriate) will be released.

  • Preregistration: OSF registrations (private → public on submission/acceptance)
  • Reproducibility: analysis scripts and codebooks shared post-acceptance
  • Data sharing: de-identified aggregates where ethics permits
  • Instrument release: final measure PDF and manual with license & required citation
